Dusk was arriving fast and we had little time to rest. A huge explosion erupted from one of our command posts, obliterating the staff in a orange ball of flame. The orks will bleed for that. The Orks came at us as they always do, yelling, revving engines, and blasting guns in the air. By now it was apparent that the Orks didn't possess any real tactical battle sense, instead they just came at you in a wave and either crashed through you or were held in check.

One our right flank, the Ork units plowed through through two Guard units dispatching all to the Emperor's paradise. It looked like were we in serious trouble on that flank but a combination of Demolisher, Griffon, and obstinate Guard shooting cut down most of the Orks. Those that survived our shooting were quickly assaulted and destroyed and our right flank was secure.

In the center, a pack of stormboyz assaulted a Hellhound and placed anti-tank charges all over the beast. It went up in a huge ball of flame and smoke, blowing shrapnel and burning fuel in a wide arc. Many of the stormboyz were caught in the explosion and died instantly as well as some of our men. The surviving stormboyz picked themselves up off the ground and immediately jumped towards our Basilisk and destroyed it too, though not as spectacularly. We dispatched the rest of the stormboyz with our lasguns and heavy bolters.

In the center of the battlefield, our troops were dropping left and right. The Orks were really strong in the center, being supported by traks, truks, buggies, dreadnoughts, and looted tanks. The Orks were slowly pushing us back into the outer reaches of the Hive and it was there we would have to make a strong stand or all of Helreach could be doomed.

We fell back and prepared better defensive positions and quickly ate a cold meal. We worked on fortifying our defenses by piling doors, barrels, and anything else we could get our hands on, forming barriers for the ork advance and cover for us. The Orks would enter a maelstrom of death and destruction of not which one would survive.

After about three hours of preparation, the Orks began sneaking through the ruins. We didn't give them too much opportunity and opened fire as soon as we could. It was a decisive moment as some Orks became confused and milled about, others broke and ran, and some came at us. Of the units that came at us, the Koptas kept on going past us not to be seen again. Did they have an alterior motive such as disrupting our supply lines or just creating more havoc than there was already? It didn't matter, they were gone and we had Orks in front of us that we needed to kill. We fired into them and they dropped, only to have the open spots fill with more Orks. The Praetorians held a strong center and didn't let the Orks get too close. After about 30 minutes the Orks began to fall back and started regrouping just out of range. We lobbed a few mortar shells into them to keep them disrupted and sent the stormtroopers ahead to report on their activity.

After monitoring the Orks for over an hour, the stormtroopers reported that the Orks were again massing in strength and preparing for a fresh attack. In our minds, we knew this was the case and it was not reassuring to hear it from the stormtroopers.

Within the hour, the Orks again came at us, a lucky shot from their Russ destroying our Basilisk before it had the chance to get a single shot off. We fought on regardless, like men possessed, and we knew that we were the last line of defense for many miles. We sold our lives dearly, and made the Orks pay for every inch of ground they took. But the Orks came at us ignoring the piles of casualties they had taken and entered melee with our boys. With burning hatred in our eyes and grim determination on our faces, the few survivors, and I mean the few, radioed High Command to explain our situation and fell back as fast as we could in our remaining Chimeras to our final battleline, 5 miles in the rear.
