Ork Forces

Warlord Morbad's Big Gunz

For years, the Orks of Warlord Morbad's Big Gunz have had an intense rivalry with those fighting for Warlord Thugsnik. The enmity the two armies have for each other reaches its greatest pitch between the two Warlords themselves. Originating from the same distant world deep in Orkdom, both Warlords matched each other battle for battle, victory for victory in every campaign they fought. Warlord Thugsnik was always the more aggressive of the two and Morbad continually suffered from persistent raids that were aimed to destroy or steal the huge artillery pieces he had ordered his Meks to build. He managed to keep pace with raids of his own, as well as bullying many lesser Ork warriors to join his army, rather than that of Thugsnik's. The rivalry reached new heights when Ghazghkull recruited both Warlords for his attack on Armageddon, as Thugsnik and Morbad both immediately set out to prove themselves in the eyes of their new overlord.

It may have been one of Ghazghkull's rare mistakes to place both Thugsnik and Morbad within a few decks of each other on the space hulk Gork Blasta for the long journey to Armageddon, as running battles between the Orks of each Warlord were common. Morbad lost several of his prized siege guns to Thugsnik's cunning raids, severely damaging his standing amongst his own Nobz. Desperate to retain a measure of credibility, Morbad bribed and threatened the Meks operating the huge teleporter arrays on board the Gork Blasta, intent on having them place Warlord Thugsnik's Big Gunz far into the wilderness of Armageddon when the planetary landings began. He was soon to find that Thugsnik had beaten him to this ploy and it was Morbad who was forced to march his army through the trackless ash wastes, desperately trying to rejoin Ghazghkull's army as Thugsnik covered himself in glory with the sheer weight of firepower in his force.

All of this changed when the Storm Troopers of the 17th Spear Points delivered a devastating assault against Warlord Thugsnik's army, destroying nearly half of the Ork's force. The balance of power has now shifted radically in Morbad's favour and it is becoming clear that he intends to take every opportunity to crush his old rival whilst his army is so much larger and Ghazghkull's eyes are turned elsewhere.

Ork Big Gunz Warbands for Epic 40,000

There are a very small number of Orks who have a special affinity for deafening noise and huge explosions, far beyond that of the rest of their species. Like minded Orks often naturally congregate together by some unknown instinct, much as those in Kult of Speed do, and so it is little surprise that these Orks tend to form their own Shooty Mobs. A talented and visionary Warboss may actively seek to recruit such mobs into his growing warband as his many Meks busy themselves with the construction of crude but devastatingly destructive siege guns. When his warband finally takes to the battlefield, it will be armed to the teeth with the most powerful weapons known to the Ork race and will be fully capable of battering any other army into utter submission.

Siege Gunz

Ork Meks are rarely satisfied with the weapons of destruction they construct. There is always a thought lurking at the back of their minds that whatever they build could be made bigger, or faster or more powerful. The development of this way of thinking is apparent in all the vehicles, weapons and war machines that roll out of the workshops of Meks throughout Orkdom.

Siege Gunz are just such an invention, taking the design of the Orks Big Gunz a step further. Using incredibly large barrels that fire high speed shells filled with a Mek's own unique explosive, the Siege Gunz have the ability to crush enemy armour or fortifications alike. They are hugely inefficient, but that matters little to the Orks who use these weapons, as they revel in the immense noise and concussion the Gunz create when they fire, as well as the huge explosion of the shells destroying their target.

In Battle

You will quickly notice that Big Gunz Warbands tend to be weaker than the normal Ork Warband and will be very vulnerable to assault. This is purely intentional and balances out some of the firepower that a detachment like this can put out. You are perhaps better off using these Big Gunz Warbands only when you know that you will be facing heavily armoured enemies and then only in relatively small sizes. Small Big Gunz Warbands supporting your larger detachments of Orks as they march into battle will force an enemy to split his attack on your entire army and the range of these Warbands will, with careful positioning, ensure that you have little to worry about from assault.

You are welcome to try more specialised warbands Ð the ability to take a complete detachment of Flakwagons is not a mistake. I just like the idea of dozens of Orks firing as much ammunition as they possibly can high into the air!
