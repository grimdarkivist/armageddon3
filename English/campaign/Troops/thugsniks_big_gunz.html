+++ Re: Raid on Ork artillery

+++ Thought: One man can do the work of an entire legion.

The raid you conducted on Ork artillery position Epsilon-Eight-Three outside Infernus Hive has been studied in detail by several of my agents and we believe we have detected a supreme opportunity to weaken the Orks' positions further. Whilst the benefit of destroying a quarter of the Orks' artillery in the area is obvious, there appears to be an entirely unintentional boon in your action.

Whilst it is relatively common knowledge that the Orks are typically a fractious race, it has been proved time and again that a charismatic leader can bind disparate warbands into a cohesive force, something that the Warlord Ghazghkull excels at. It is also apparent, though, that these temporary alliances can be fragile at best and it is in this that you have succeeded in driving a wedge between two rival Warlords under Ghazghkull's direct command. This provides us with a situation that I fully intend to take advantage of and I require your company to be ready to take possible action at a moment's notice.

Through the study of the Orks' deployment around Infernus, we have isolated two artillery forces led by Warlords Thugsnik and Morbad who, we have learnt, are bitter rivals, each keen to demonstrate their power and relative worth to their leader, Ghazghkull himself.

During your admirable attack on Warlord Thugsnik's ammunition dumps, you managed to annihilate almost a full half of his forces. This has had the effect of seriously altering the balance of power within Ghazghkull's forces as, we foresee, Warlord Morbad will be very opportunistic towards the immense loss his rival has suffered.

A number of my agents have already infiltrated Morbad's camp with the aim of launching a series of disruptive attacks that will, for all intents and purposes, appear to show that Thugsnik is attempting to save face amongst his own warriors. We hope that this should further push Warlord Morbad into taking action against his old rival. At worst, we may find that the big guns of the Orks are turned away from Infernus Hive for a few vital hours. At best, we may see the eradication of one Ork army and the weakening of another if Morbad can be provoked into launching a full scale attack on Thugsnik.

The danger to this elegant plan is that Ghazghkull is renowned for his ability to dominate his underlings, forcing them to concentrate on his goals rather than their own petty ends. In part, this is where your company may play a part, keeping Ghazghkull off balance with a series of devastating raids whilst two of his Warlords tear each other apart. If my plan comes to fruition, your forces may also be required to finish off the army of the victorious Warlord Morbad, presumably, though I have learnt not to consider absolutes when it comes to war with the Ork species.

Be ready.

Ork Big Gunz Warbands for Epic 40,000

There are a very small number of Orks who have a special affinity for deafening noise and huge explosions, far beyond that of the rest of their species. Like minded Orks often naturally congregate together by some unknown instinct, much as those in Kult of Speed do, and so it is little surprise that these Orks tend to form their own Shooty Mobs. A talented and visionary Warboss may actively seek to recruit such mobs into his growing warband as his many Meks busy themselves with the construction of crude but devastatingly destructive siege guns. When his warband finally takes to the battlefield, it will be armed to the teeth with the most powerful weapons known to the Ork race and will be fully capable of battering any other army into utter submission.

Siege Gunz

Ork Meks are rarely satisfied with the weapons of destruction they construct. There is always a thought lurking at the back of their minds that whatever they build could be made bigger, or faster or more powerful. The development of this way of thinking is apparent in all the vehicles, weapons and war machines that roll out of the workshops of Meks throughout Orkdom.

Siege Gunz are just such an invention, taking the design of the Orks Big Gunz a step further. Using incredibly large barrels that fire high speed shells filled with a Mek's own unique explosive, the Siege Gunz have the ability to crush enemy armour or fortifications alike. They are hugely inefficient, but that matters little to the Orks who use these weapons, as they revel in the immense noise and concussion the Gunz create when they fire, as well as the huge explosion of the shells destroying their target.

In Battle

You will quickly notice that Big Gunz Warbands tend to be weaker than the normal Ork Warband and will be very vulnerable to assault. This is purely intentional and balances out some of the firepower that a detachment like this can put out. You are perhaps better off using these Big Gunz Warbands only when you know that you will be facing heavily armoured enemies and then only in relatively small sizes. Small Big Gunz Warbands supporting your larger detachments of Orks as they march into battle will force an enemy to split his attack on your entire army and the range of these Warbands will, with careful positioning, ensure that you have little to worry about from assault.

You are welcome to try more specialised warbands Ð the ability to take a complete detachment of Flakwagons is not a mistake. I just like the idea of dozens of Orks firing as much ammunition as they possibly can high into the air!
